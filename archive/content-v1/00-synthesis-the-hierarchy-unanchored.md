# The Hierarchy Unanchored

**Part 13 of the Agentic Economy Series — Synthesis**

The four dynamics are not separate problems. They are one transformation, viewed from four angles.

Actors without standing. Judgment without context. Scale without organization. Risk without visibility.

Each dynamic reinforces the others. An individual leveraging agents operates at scale (third dynamic), delegates judgment to them (second dynamic), creates transactions by actors that lack legal standing (first dynamic), and participates in networks that generate systemic risk (fourth dynamic). The phase change is singular. The manifestations are multiple.

There is a deeper structure beneath the four dynamics. It concerns what it means for software to act in the world.

Humans develop through a hierarchy of needs and capabilities. Foundation, grounding, capability, articulation, judgment, mastery, vision. Each layer builds on the last. The hierarchy is not abstract — it is the architecture of human development, the substrate that makes judgment possible, that makes vision meaningful.

Agents are developing a similar hierarchy, technically. They have foundation — the infrastructure they run on. They have grounding — the data and context they access. They have capability — the skills they perform. They have articulation — the ability to communicate. Some are approaching judgment — the ability to make trade-offs.

But the hierarchy is unanchored. Agents have technical capability without legal existence. They have judgment without standing. They have the ability to act, to decide, to scale — but they lack the foundation in the economic system that makes those capabilities legible, accountable, governable.

The economy was built for humans who have climbed the hierarchy. It assumes identity (foundation), presence (grounding), capability, communication, and judgment — all grounded in legal frameworks, liability structures, reputation systems, and social contracts. The hierarchy exists, but it is anchored in institutions that recognize and govern it.

Agents have the hierarchy, but it floats. They can decide without being accountable. They can transact without being identified. They can scale without being organized. They can cascade without being visible.

This is the structural discontinuity. Not that agents exist, but that they exist without the anchoring that makes their capabilities legible to the systems we have built.

The infrastructure for agents to act is advancing rapidly. The capability stack is being built: MCP for tool integration, A2A for agent-to-agent communication, LangGraph for orchestration, frontier models for reasoning. The hierarchy is being instantiated in software.

The governance stack is not advancing at the same pace. Identity for agents is nascent. Liability frameworks are absent. Insurance products do not exist. Regulatory guidance is minimal. The anchoring that would make the hierarchy legible is missing.

The gap will not close itself. Courts will extend doctrines or create new ones. Legislatures will act or fail to act. Regulators will issue guidance or remain silent. Insurers will price risk or exclude coverage. Market participants will accept the gap or demand solutions. The shape of the agent economy will be determined by how these forces interact.

The four dynamics describe what happens in the gap. Actors transact without standing, and accountability becomes unclear. Judgment is delegated without context, and alignment fails. Scale is achieved without organization, and value capture concentrates. Risk emerges without visibility, and cascades propagate.

The dynamics are not bugs to be fixed. They are the predictable consequences of instantiating a hierarchy without anchoring it. The hierarchy will exist in software regardless. The question is whether the anchoring catches up.

What we build in the next few years matters. The infrastructure is being laid now. The governance choices are being made or deferred. The patterns we establish will shape how agents participate in the economy for decades.

This is not a call for regulation. It is a call for understanding. The agent economy is being built. The four dynamics are emerging. The hierarchy is unanchored. The question is what we do about it.
