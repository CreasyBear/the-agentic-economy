**2/**
The entire architecture of economic life — firms, markets, laws, governance — was built around one constraint:

Human attention is finite.

A business can only serve as many customers as it has people. A professional can only hold so many relationships. An investor can only evaluate so many decisions before judgment degrades.

**3/**
Now imagine that constraint lifts.

Software begins to act — not just respond. It perceives, forms intentions, plans, executes, and adapts. Without a human directing each step.

This isn't hypothetical. The infrastructure exists. It's being deployed. Now.

**4/**
A person with agents isn't merely more productive.

They're a different kind of economic participant.

They operate at a scale previously available only to organisations. They negotiate simultaneously in a dozen markets. They don't sleep. Their attention doesn't deplete.

**5/**
Now extend: not one person with agents. Everyone with agents.

Each acting in the name of its principal — buying, selling, committing, coordinating — at machine speed, across every domain, in every jurisdiction.

The economy that forms isn't the current economy with better tools. It's a structurally different system.
